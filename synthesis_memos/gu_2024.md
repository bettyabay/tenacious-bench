# Memo: Gu et al., 2024 — LLM-as-a-Judge Survey

**Paper:** "A Survey on LLM-as-a-Judge" — Gu et al., 2024
**Relevance:** Primary justification for Path B (preference-tuned judge architecture)

---

## Key Claims

- Preference-tuned judges outperform zero-shot judges on binary classification tasks
- Clear positive/negative examples are sufficient for reliable judge training (< 500 pairs)
- Judges generalise better when trained on domain-specific (chosen, rejected) pairs
- Position bias and verbosity bias are the primary failure modes of LLM judges

## Application to This Project

- Binary judgment (suppress/escalate vs send) matches the task structure where preference-tuned judges excel
- 200-300 pairs is within the effective range identified in the survey
- Domain-specific pairs (B2B sales outreach) reduce judge hallucination on signal interpretation
- Temperature=0.0 and short output format (JSON) mitigate verbosity bias

## Caveats

- Survey covers general-purpose judges; sales outreach domain not specifically studied
- Preference-tuned judges may still fail on novel disqualifier types not in training set
